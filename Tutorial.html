Volley

In Blue Lock Rivals, performing a volley is a special technique that allows your character to score goals directly off an assist without letting the ball touch the ground. To successfully execute a volley, you first need to have a striker who has a volley skill or synergy that supports volley-type plays. During the match simulation, you'll need to build team synergy and pass combinations that set up a volley opportunity. Typically, this means having a midfielder or winger with an assist skill that triggers a volley chance. Once the conditions are right, and if the striker's volley skill activates, you'll see a cinematic or highlighted animation showing the character executing the move. Timing, team composition, and skill synergy are key factors in triggering and succeeding with volleys. Make sure to level up the striker and support players to increase the odds of the skill activating during the game.

Volley(generic)

In Blue Lock Rivals, performing a volley without relying on a character’s special ability is all about team setup and match simulation conditions. To trigger a volley naturally, you need to create a play where the striker receives a pass in midair—such as a cross or a chip pass—without the ball touching the ground. This can happen during normal gameplay if you use midfielders or wingers with high passing stats to deliver the ball into the box, and a striker with solid shooting and positioning stats waiting in front of goal. The key is to ensure the assist is fast or lofted, so the striker reacts with a first-time shot before the ball lands. Even without an equipped volley skill, the game will sometimes animate a basic volley shot based on stats, positioning, and timing. By building plays that emphasize aerial assists or quick passing combinations, you can naturally trigger a volley and increase your chances of scoring stylish goals.

Header

In Blue Lock: Rivals, doing a header is simple and manual—you don’t need to set up formations or rely on abilities. When you're in a match and your character receives a cross or lofted ball, just tap the shoot button while jumping to perform a header. The jump and shoot action together trigger the header animation if your character is in the right position near the ball. You don’t need any special timing system or stat setup—just make sure you jump into the ball and shoot as it comes toward you. That’s it. It’s all about good positioning and pressing jump + shoot at the right moment.

Goalkeeping

In Blue Lock: Rivals, when you're controlling the goalkeeper during a match, your job is to react quickly and block incoming shots using timing and positioning. When the opponent shoots, you’ll see a red circle or aiming indicator showing where the ball is going. To save the shot, you need to drag your finger or swipe toward that direction and tap at the right time to dive and make the save. The timing is critical — too early or too late and you’ll miss. If the shot is close to the center, just tapping quickly might be enough, but for corner shots, you’ll need to swipe further to reach it. The closer the ball is to the edge of the goal, the faster your reaction needs to be. There’s no special stat to rely on — it’s all about reflexes and practice. Also, some shots come with a charge or animation — in these cases, pay attention to the wind-up and be ready to commit to a direction just before the shot is released. Mastering goalkeeping in BLR means staying calm, reading the shooter, and practicing your reaction timing to consistently block even fast, tricky shots.
